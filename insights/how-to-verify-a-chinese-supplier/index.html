How to Verify a Chinese Supplier: A 2026 Checklist

Choosing the wrong supplier is the most common — and most expensive — sourcing mistake. Use this checklist to confirm a supplier is real, capable, and the right fit before you commit.

1. Confirm legitimacy

  • Verify the business license and registration details
  • Check that the company name, bank account, and contact match
  • Confirm whether they are a manufacturer or a trading company

2. Assess capability

  • Confirm they actually make your product category (not just resell it)
  • Review capacity, equipment, and typical lead times
  • Ask for references or comparable export experience

3. Check compliance

  • Request certifications relevant to your destination market
  • Confirm material and specification documentation

4. Validate with samples

  • Order samples and test against your quality requirements
  • Judge responsiveness and communication during sampling

FAQ

Verifying suppliers — FAQ

Is a business license enough to trust a supplier?

No. A business license confirms a company exists, but you also need to confirm manufacturing capability, product fit, certifications, and sample quality. Legitimacy and capability are different things.

Should I always ask for samples?

Yes. Samples validate quality, materials, and specifications before a production order, and they reveal how responsive and capable a supplier really is.
