FAQ

OEM / ODM sourcing — FAQ

What is the difference between OEM and ODM?

OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) means the factory builds a product to your design and specifications. ODM (Original Design Manufacturer) means you adapt an existing factory design — adding your branding, packaging, or minor changes. OEM gives more control; ODM is usually faster and lower-cost.

Can you manage private label and custom packaging?

Yes. We coordinate private label, custom packaging, labeling, and specification requirements with the manufacturer, including sample rounds before production.

Do you help negotiate MOQ?

Yes. MOQ negotiation is part of OEM/ODM project management, alongside pricing, tooling, lead times, and sampling.
