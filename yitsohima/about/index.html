Who is Charu?

Charu is the spirit at the heart of our atelier — part wanderer, part keeper of old symbols, wholly devoted to the high country and the hands that craft within it.

The beginning

A craft worth carrying forward

Himalaya Atelier grew from a season spent among nomadic herders, where every object — a sling, a bead, a knot of yak wool — held both a use and a meaning. We set out to honour that union of craft and intention, and to give those traditions a place in everyday life beyond the mountains.

We design with restraint, work in small batches, and let the symbol lead. Nothing is mass-produced; everything is made to be kept.

Our promise

Nomadic sustainability

We use recycled metals, ethically gathered natural materials, and plastic-free packaging. A share of every sale returns to the artisan communities and the landscapes that inspire our work — so the craft, and the place it comes from, can endure.
